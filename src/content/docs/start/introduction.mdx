**Prevail** (styled `prevAIl`) is a terminal cockpit for the rest of your life.

Every part of your life — wealth, health, tax, career — is a folder of markdown. You open one, ask a question, and Prevail fans that question out to every AI CLI you already have logged in: [Claude Code](https://claude.com/code), [Codex](https://github.com/openai/codex), [Antigravity](https://github.com/google/antigravity-cli) (Google's `agy`), and your local [Ollama](https://ollama.com). They answer in parallel. A **chair** model reads all the replies and writes one decisive answer — with a dedicated panel surfacing where the panelists *disagreed*, which is the point.

It runs from your terminal, or from your phone over a Telegram bridge. It ships as a single ~95 MB binary. No daemon, no Docker, no API keys to manage.

## The three ideas

Prevail is built on three concepts. The rest of these docs are just detail on top of them.

### 1. The council

Ask one question; get answers from up to four different models at once; let a chair synthesize a single verdict. A single model is one voice with one set of blind spots. A **council** turns disagreement into signal — when Claude says "rent" and Codex says "sell," the *reason they diverge* is usually the actual decision you need to make.

→ [How the council works](/council/overview/)

### 2. The vault

Your data is not in a database or a cloud. It's a folder. Each **domain** — `wealth/`, `health/`, `tax/` — is a subfolder of plain markdown files you can edit in any editor, grep from the shell, and sync with git, iCloud, or Tailscale. Prevail reads it as context and writes back a self-curating decision log. You own every byte.

→ [Vault structure](/vault/structure/)

### 3. The cockpit

The interface that ties them together: a TUI (terminal UI) with a sidebar of your life domains and connected apps on the left, a per-domain workspace on the right, and a single **ConfigBar** along the bottom carrying every per-turn dial — Council, Framework, Lens, Web, Save, Serendipity, Auto.

→ [Cockpit layout](/cockpit/layout/)

## What makes it different

| | |
|---|---|
| **Council, not a single voice** | Four models in parallel, one chair synthesizes. Disagreement gets its own panel. |
| **Domain = folder** | Plain markdown. Edit anywhere. Sync however you like. No database. |
| **Uses CLIs you already pay for** | Spawns `claude`, `codex`, `agy`, `ollama` — inherits every login, MCP server, and skill. No new API keys. |
| **Local-private when it matters** | Ollama auto-detected. Run sensitive domains on a council that never leaves your machine. |
| **Off the keyboard** | `prevail daemon --telegram` exposes the cockpit on your phone, chat-ID allowlist enforced. |
| **Scheduled briefings** | A verdict on "what's new in wealth this week?" lands on your phone at 7am. |
| **Self-curating vault** | Every chat writes a one-line summary to `<domain>/_log/`. The vault writes its own decision log. |
| **Hardened by default** | Env-scrubbed subprocesses, prompt-injection–aware operating manual, OAuth tokens `chmod 0600`. |

## What Prevail is *not*

Prevail is a **single-user terminal cockpit** for personal decisions. You run it on your own machine, with your own AI CLI credentials, against a folder of your own markdown.

It is **not** a SaaS, **not** multi-tenant, **not** an enterprise audit-log system, **not** a replacement for a knowledge base. If you need any of those, Prevail is the wrong tool. Being deliberately small is the point — it keeps your data on your disk and your trust boundary at your own laptop.

## Requirements at a glance

- One or more of: Claude Code, Codex, Antigravity (`agy`), or Ollama — installed and on your `$PATH`.
- A terminal with UTF-8 and true color.
- macOS or Linux (Windows via WSL).

Prevail brings no models of its own. It spawns the CLIs you already have, inheriting every login and configuration.
